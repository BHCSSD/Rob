In this lab, you will use two IR sensors pointed at the ground to detect a black line on a white surface.  
Your robot must interpret these "light vs. dark" readings to stay on track.

---

# Level 1: The "Digital Eyes" (Status Reporting)

Before moving the motors, your robot needs to "know" where it is.  
We will use the Serial Monitor to debug our sensors.

## The Goal
Calibrate your sensors so they can clearly tell the difference between:
- Black tape
- White floor

## The Logic
Program your robot to print its status to the Serial Monitor based on the four possible states:

| Left Sensor | Right Sensor | Serial Message |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| White | White | `"Off the Line"` |
| White | Black | `"Veering Right"` <br> *(The line is under the left side)* |
| Black | White | `"Veering Left"` <br> *(The line is under the right side)* |
| Black | Black | `"On the Line"` |

---

# Level 2: Reactive Steering

Now, translate those Serial messages into motor movements.

## The Goal
Keep the robot centered on the line by counter-steering.

## The Logic

- **Both on Black**  Move Forward
- **Left on Black**  Robot is drifting right  Turn Left
- **Right on Black**  Robot is drifting left  Turn Right
- **Both on White**  Robot has lost the line  Stop or Backtrack

---

# Level 3: Smooth Navigation & Error Handling

Instead of fixing a jittery robot with complex code, you will use hardware engineering to smooth out the ride.  
You will also add a safety timer to prevent your robot from driving away if it loses the track.

---

## Task A: Designing a Physical Deadband

If your sensors are too close together, they will both hit the black line at the same time, causing the robot to stutter.

### The Fix
Adjust the width of your IR sensors:
- Move them further apart.
- The black line should fit cleanly between them.

### The Result
This creates a Physical Deadband:
- When perfectly centered: both sensors see White.
- The robot only turns when one sensor trips over the line.

### Hardware Challenge: Find the "Sweet Spot"

- Too wide  robot reacts too late.
- Too narrow  robot wiggles constantly.

Experiment and refine your placement.

---

## Task B: The "Lost Robot" Timer (Fail-Safe)

If the robot misses a sharp turn and both sensors see white for too long, it should stop instead of wandering away.

### The Logic
Use:
- A timer  
  **or**
- A counter variable  

to track how long the robot has been in the "Both White" state.

### The Requirement

If:

```cpp
Left == White && Right == White
````

for more than 1.0 second,
kill power to both motors.

# Level 4: The "Search Pattern" bonus
Before giving up and stopping, can you make your robot hunt for the line?

## Challenge

If the line is lost:

* Rotate left slightly.
* Rotate right slightly.
* Create a small "wiggle" pattern.

Attempt to rediscover the black path before the 2-second timer expires.
